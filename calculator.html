THE GREEKS — OPTION SENSITIVITY MEASURES
Δ Delta
PRICE SENSITIVITY
Rate of change in option price for every $1 move in the underlying. A delta of 0.40 means the option gains ~$0.40 per $1 the stock rises.
CALL: 0 to +1 PUT: -1 to 0
At-the-money options have delta near ±0.50. Deep ITM approaches ±1.00. Far OTM approaches 0. Delta also approximates the probability the option expires ITM.
θ Theta
TIME DECAY
Dollar amount the option loses per day as expiration approaches, all else equal. A theta of -0.05 means the option loses $5 per day per contract.
LONG: negative (costs you) SHORT: positive (earns you)
Theta accelerates in the final 30 DTE — the last month loses value fastest. Sellers of credit spreads benefit from theta. Buyers fight it every day.
ν Vega
VOLATILITY SENSITIVITY
Dollar change in option price for every 1% change in implied volatility. A vega of 0.08 means the option gains $8 if IV rises 1%.
LONG: positive (IV up = profit) SHORT: negative (IV up = loss)
Vega is highest for ATM options and longer DTE. Selling into high IV earns inflated premium — the core edge of credit spread strategies.
Γ Gamma
DELTA ACCELERATION
Rate of change of delta for every $1 move in the stock. High gamma means delta shifts rapidly — the option becomes more directional quickly.
LONG: positive gamma SHORT: negative gamma
Gamma spikes near expiration for ATM options. Short gamma (credit spreads) is dangerous close to expiry — a sharp move can rapidly increase losses.
ρ Rho
INTEREST RATE SENSITIVITY
Change in option price for every 1% change in the risk-free interest rate. Generally the smallest Greek in practice for short-dated options.
CALL: positive rho PUT: negative rho
Rho matters most for long-dated options (LEAPS). For 30–60 DTE positions the effect is minimal. The risk-free rate slider in OPTS// accounts for this.
IV Rank
VOLATILITY CONTEXT
Where current IV sits relative to its 52-week range. IV Rank of 80 means IV is in the top 20% of its historical range — elevated.
HIGH (>50): sell premium LOW (<30): buy premium
High IV Rank = inflated options premiums = favorable for credit spread sellers. Low IV Rank = cheap options = favorable for debit spread buyers.
Position summary: Credit spread sellers want low delta (OTM strike), positive theta (time passing), negative vega (IV falling after entry), and low gamma (no surprise moves). Debit spread buyers want the opposite on all four.

POSITION PARAMETERS
PREMIUM ($)iThe price you pay (debit) or collect (credit) per share when entering the trade. Multiply by 100 for the per-contract dollar amount.
WIDTH ($)iThe distance between your two strike prices in a spread. Wider spreads offer more profit potential but also more risk.
STRIKEiThe price at which your option gives the right to buy (call) or sell (put) the stock. For spreads, this is your short strike — the one you sell.
CALL / PUTiCALL profits when the stock rises. PUT profits when the stock falls. Choose based on your directional outlook.
DTE (DAYS)iDays To Expiration — how many days until the option contract expires. More DTE = more time for the trade to work, but slower time decay.
IMPLIED VOLATILITYiThe market's expectation of future price movement expressed as a percentage. High IV = expensive options. Sellers prefer high IV, buyers prefer low IV. 75%
